# Demo Video Script — Records Request Assistant

## Target audience

Patients who want to request their medical records but aren't tech-savvy. Assume no familiarity with Chrome extensions, Claude, or browser automation.

## Setup (before recording)

- Chrome open, signed in to Claude
- Claude in Chrome extension NOT installed (so you can show the install flow)
- Patient Gateway account ready to log in
- Claude Code or Claude Desktop open

---

## Part 1: Install Claude in Chrome (~1 min)

**[Show Chrome browser, toolbar visible]**

"To get started, we need to add a free tool called Claude in Chrome to your browser. I'll show you exactly how."

1. Click the puzzle piece icon (Extensions) in the Chrome toolbar
2. Click "Manage Extensions" at the bottom
3. Click "Open Chrome Web Store" (or navigate to chrome.google.com/webstore)
4. Type "Claude" in the search bar
5. Find "Claude" by Anthropic — click "Add to Chrome"
6. Click "Add extension" in the confirmation dialog
7. The Claude icon appears in the toolbar

"That's it — Claude is now installed in your browser."

**[Click the Claude icon, sign in if needed]**

"Sign in with your Claude account. If you don't have one, you can create a free account at claude.ai."

---

## Part 2: Log in to your patient portal (~30 sec)

**[Navigate to Patient Gateway]**

"Now go to your patient portal and log in the way you normally do. The assistant will never see or touch your password — you handle the login yourself."

1. Navigate to patientgateway.massgeneralbrigham.org
2. Log in with your credentials
3. Complete any 2FA/security questions
4. Arrive at the home page ("Welcome, Adrian!")

"Once you're on your home page, you're ready to start."

---

## Part 3: Start the assistant (~30 sec)

**[Switch to Claude Code / Claude Desktop]**

"Now switch to Claude and ask it to help you request your records."

Type something like: "Help me request my medical records from the Patient Gateway page I have open."

**[Assistant connects to the Chrome tab]**

"The assistant can now see your portal page — but only because you gave it permission. It can't access anything else on your computer."

---

## Part 4: Navigate to the form (~1 min)

**[Show the browser as the assistant works]**

The assistant says: "I can see you're logged in to Mass General Brigham Patient Gateway. I'll open the menu to find the records request form."

1. **[Hamburger menu opens]** — the assistant clicks the menu
2. **[Scrolls to "Request Records"]** — the assistant finds it under "My Record"
3. **[Navigates to the form]**

The assistant says: "I found the request form at Menu → My Record → Request Records. This is the Release of Information page. Is this the right place?"

**[User confirms]**

---

## Part 5: Fill out the form (~2 min)

### Organization

The assistant says: "Select your organization from the dropdown below."

**[Dropdown opens, user clicks "Mass General Brigham"]**

### Recipient

The assistant says: "Who should the records be sent to — you, or someone else?"

**[User selects "Me"]**

### Date range

The assistant says: "I'll set the date range to the past 6 months — December 7, 2025 through June 7, 2026. Does that look right?"

**[User confirms, dates are filled in]**

### Locations

The assistant says: "Select which hospital locations to include."

**[User checks their locations]**

### Record types

The assistant says: "I'll include all record types except 'Other.' OK?"

**[User confirms, checkboxes are checked]**

### Continue

The assistant says: "Everything looks good on this page. Ready to continue to the review step?"

**[User confirms, Continue is clicked]**

---

## Part 6: Review and submit (~1 min)

**[Review page loads]**

The assistant says: "Here's a summary of your request:
- Organization: Mass General Brigham
- Sent to: You
- Date range: 12/7/2025 – 6/7/2026
- Location: Massachusetts General Hospital
- Record types: All except Other

There's a consent section that says your records may include sensitive information and you should review them before sharing. The authorization expires in 90 days.

Would you like to submit this request?"

**[User confirms]**

The assistant says: "Submitting your request now."

**[Send request is clicked]**

**[Confirmation page]**

The assistant says: "Your request has been submitted. You should receive your records within [timeframe]. I've saved the details so next time will be even faster."

---

## Closing (~15 sec)

"That's it — your records have been requested. Next time you need to do this, the assistant will remember your preferences and the date of this request, so it can pick up right where you left off."

---

## Production Notes

- **Total estimated length:** 5–6 minutes
- **Key moments to emphasize:** The assistant always asks before doing anything. The patient stays in control.
- **Tone:** Calm, unhurried, encouraging. This is for people who may be anxious about technology touching their health records.
- **Captions:** Add captions — accessibility matters, and some viewers will watch without sound.
- **Resolution:** Record at 1080p minimum. The form fields need to be readable.
